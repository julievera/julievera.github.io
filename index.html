About Me

I'm an HCI researcher and UX professional with a focus on how people make sense of information during crisis events. My dissertation research explores coordination and sensemaking within YouTube livestreams during severe weather events, examining how forecasters and viewers collaborate to understand and respond to emerging threats.

With over a decade of experience in marketing, advertising, and UX research, I bridge academic rigor with practical industry applications. I'm passionate about designing user experiences that help people navigate complex information landscapes, particularly in high-stakes situations.
