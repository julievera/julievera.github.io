Current research

Why people return to unofficial weather interpreters — and how trust grows, breaks, or changes over time.

I’m extending my dissertation work on weatherfluencers into questions of trust, distrust, authority, and community in severe weather information systems.

The next phase looks beyond single livestreams to why audiences return across events, how creators become familiar or suspect over time, and how local observation, ground-truth reporting, community infrastructure, and repeated interpretive performance shape public reliance during severe weather.

The broader question is how and why people come to trust non-official or ambiguously credentialed communicators in domains where expertise is supposed to be institutional. Severe weather is the starting point, but the pattern extends to news, creators, influencers, and other high-stakes public information environments.

Research in practice

In industry, I build research systems and lead studies across product portfolios where teams need to make better decisions with incomplete evidence.

At AutoNation, this has meant building UX Research infrastructure while leading work across ecommerce, vehicle discovery, selling/trade-in experiences, content strategy, photography standards, AI-assisted shopping tools, and customer confidence in high-consideration automotive decisions.

Research infrastructure and operating systems Building study templates, intake norms, participant screening practices, reusable reporting formats, research roadmaps, and shared artifacts so research has institutional memory and product visibility.
Trust and confidence in automotive decisions Studying how shoppers and sellers interpret offers, next steps, data-sharing requests, vehicle details, dealership expectations, and signals of legitimacy in high-consideration digital experiences.
Search, discovery, and content interpretation Understanding how customers use search, filters, vehicle detail pages, educational content, terminology, and AI-assisted explanations to make sense of complex purchase options.
Visual credibility and merchandising quality Studying how photography, image consistency, editing, backgrounds, distortions, and vehicle condition cues shape perceived transparency and willingness to keep shopping.
