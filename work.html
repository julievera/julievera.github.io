Applied Research

Domains and problem types I have industry experience with, not a project archive. Detailed process, methods, and findings are reserved for interviews.

AI-generated content and trust calibration I help teams understand where AI-authored content creates confidence, where it creates skepticism, and what kinds of claims require proof, provenance, or human accountability.

Experience includes research on AI-generated vehicle summaries, content hierarchy, disclosure expectations, hallucination risk, and high-stakes purchase decisions.

What this demonstrates: ability to study AI-generated content as a trust problem, not just a UI problem.
Research infrastructure for ambiguous product spaces I build research programs where teams lack shared vocabulary, foundational knowledge, or clear decision criteria.

Experience includes building AutoNation's first behavior-first consumer research framework, triangulating qualitative and quantitative methods into a shared vocabulary for trust, risk, and decision fatigue.

What this demonstrates: ability to stand up a research function from nothing and make it institutionally durable.
Search, marketplace relevance, and decision support I study how people evaluate results, credibility signals, and choice architecture in marketplace environments.

Experience includes search relevance and job-seeker decision-making research in a two-sided employment marketplace at Monster.

What this demonstrates: ability to connect perceived quality to concrete ranking and product decisions.
Concept risk and product bets I help teams understand whether an interaction model fits a user's actual decision context before investing in build.

Experience includes evaluating swipe-based job application concepts and redirecting product attention toward higher-value needs.

What this demonstrates: willingness to tell a team a popular idea is the wrong bet, backed by evidence.
Internal tools and enterprise workflows I study how field evidence about internal tools and workflows should inform engineering investment and roadmap prioritization.

Experience includes research on enterprise sales tooling and workflow systems (Amazon, AWS).

What this demonstrates: research judgment that translates outside consumer-facing contexts into internal, operational ones.
Crisis information and platform-mediated expertise I study how publics assess credibility and expertise when creators, institutions, platforms, and real-time risk collide.

Experience includes doctoral research on severe weather livestreams and weatherfluencers.

What this demonstrates: the signature case for my broader research agenda; see Research Agenda for the full argument.
