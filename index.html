The Surprising Role of TikTok in Natural Disaster Response

March 15, 2025 Crisis Communication, Social Media

In recent years, TikTok has emerged as an unexpected but powerful platform for crisis communication during natural disasters. While emergency management traditionally relies on broadcast media and official channels, our research shows that short-form video content created by everyday users serves a crucial role in information dissemination and emotional processing during crisis events.

Through our ongoing research, we've identified three key patterns in how TikTok is used during disasters:

1. Real-time documentation and witness accounts

Unlike traditional media with production delays, TikTok enables immediate distribution of first-person accounts. These raw, unfiltered perspectives provide critical on-the-ground information before official sources can respond.

2. Information synthesis and verification

Creators with no formal journalism training are developing sophisticated fact-checking methods, synthesizing information from multiple sources, and helping their communities make sense of conflicting reports.

3. Emotional processing and community support

Beyond factual information, TikTok provides a space for emotional processing where users share experiences, offer support, and develop collective understanding of traumatic events.

About Me

I'm a Lead UX Researcher and Ph.D. Candidate (ABD, est. Fall 2025) with 10+ years of experience transforming organizations through evidence-based product development. My research focuses on human-computer interaction and social computing, with particular emphasis on crisis communication, misinformation on social platforms, platform governance, and issues of trust and safety.

Research Interests

My current research explores how people make sense of information in algorithmically-curated environments, particularly on visual social media platforms like TikTok. I'm fascinated by how everyday users develop strategies to navigate complex information ecosystems during times of uncertainty and crisis.
