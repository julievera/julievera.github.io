The Surprising Role of TikTok in Natural Disaster Response

March 15, 2025 Crisis Communication, Social Media

In recent years, TikTok has emerged as an unexpected but powerful platform for crisis communication during natural disasters. While emergency management traditionally relies on broadcast media and official channels, our research shows that short-form video content created by everyday users serves a crucial role in information dissemination and emotional processing during crisis events.

Through our ongoing research, we've identified three key patterns in how TikTok is used during disasters:

1. Real-time documentation and witness accounts

Unlike traditional media with production delays, TikTok enables immediate distribution of first-person accounts. These raw, unfiltered perspectives provide critical on-the-ground information before official sources can respond.

2. Information synthesis and verification

Creators with no formal journalism training are developing sophisticated fact-checking methods, synthesizing information from multiple sources, and helping their communities make sense of conflicting reports.

3. Emotional processing and community support

Beyond factual information, TikTok provides a space for emotional processing where users share experiences, offer support, and develop collective understanding of traumatic events.

As our research continues, we're developing frameworks to help emergency management agencies better integrate these informal communication channels into their response strategies. The democratization of crisis communication presents both challenges and opportunities for building more resilient communities.

From Data to Story: Turning UX Research into Compelling Narratives

February 27, 2025 UX Research, Storytelling, Data Communication

One of the biggest challenges for UX researchers isn't collecting data—it's translating that data into stories that drive organizational change. After a decade in research roles across multiple industries, I've developed a framework for transforming complex research findings into narratives that resonate with stakeholders and inspire action.

Here are the key principles I've found most effective:

Start with the human, not the data

Research presentations often begin with methodology and sample sizes. Instead, open with a specific human story that exemplifies your key finding. This creates immediate emotional engagement and helps stakeholders see the human impact of the problem.

Structure findings as a journey

Rather than presenting disconnected insights, frame your research as a cohesive journey—with challenges, turning points, and resolution opportunities. This narrative structure helps stakeholders understand how different findings connect to form a bigger picture.

Make the invisible visible

Great research storytelling reveals what was previously hidden. Use visuals, quotes, and artifacts to bring overlooked experiences into focus, creating "aha moments" that challenge assumptions and spark new thinking.

The most impactful research isn't necessarily the most rigorous or comprehensive—it's the research that tells a compelling story and creates a shared understanding that drives action. By focusing on narrative craft alongside methodological rigor, researchers can significantly amplify their influence on product decisions.
